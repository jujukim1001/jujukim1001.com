Blue Endeavor

OVERVIEW
As one of the client request projects at Young & Hungry agency, I was in charge of conducting a redesign of the existing Blue Endeavor app. Blue Endeavor was our client, and they wanted us to pursue their business needs and do a visual refresh of their existing design.

IDEATION
Product planning
Before exploring our solutions into visual layouts, we organized the list of features that should be included in the product. Also, we created an empathy map of how users are going to interact with our product from beginning to end.
Information architecture
VALIDATION
Paper prototype test
Before moving on to hi-fidelity design, we wanted to do a usability test to validate our initial ideas and get user feedback. We asked 5 participants to perform three tasks.
• Find a room and request a chat with the poster.
• Find a roommate and request a chat.
• Start a room post completing the required steps.
Research synthesis
Result
• 80%: Successfully navigated the app.
• 60%: Clearly understood the context of the app.
• 60%: Completed the room post process.
Findings
• Navigation: Easy to find the information that users were looking for.
• Content: Got confused with the real estate app, didn’t fullu get the purpose of the app at first use.
Possible solutions
• Refine the content to give more explanation of this app.
• Prioritize the information that needs to be presented on the screens.
SOLUTION ANALYSIS
Main home: Room tab
What we iterated?
• Update the title: Title needed to be more self-explanatory. By looking at the title, users should be able to instantly understand the purpose of this app. (We are not Airbnb or a real estate app)
• Add a thumbnail of other roommates: Users want to know the full list of roommates they are sharing their room with.
• Reorganize contents: The location of the apartment and the available move-in-date has more priority than lease duration.
Main home: Livemate tab
What we iterated?
• Change content: Title needed to be more self-explanatory. Updated information that our users prefer more to see in the preview.
• Add Save Livmate option: Users could save potential livmates and send chat requests later.

Let’s Talk about the Next Step!
What I learned
How to manage a design team
Managing a team was not easy. Being a co-founder of this project team, I was involved in recruiting other team members, organizing team tasks and schedules, and creating a deck for every meeting. I learned how to communicate my ideas effectively and also to give and receive feedback. Since our team worked remotely, I learned a lot of tools to help us communicate and collaborate from different places.
Importance of documentating and organizing work
Livmate was a long-term project and we had multiple iterations from initial sketch to final prototype. Therefore, it was important to organize and manage our design files in different versions and history. This also helped us when we hand off our design files and explain our design iterations to other teammates.
What's up Next?
